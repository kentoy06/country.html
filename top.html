GRAND PLACE

The Grand Place or Grote Markt is the stunning central square of Brussels, Belgium, and is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful squares in Europe. Surrounded by opulent 17th-century guildhalls, the Brussels Town Hall, and the King’s House (Maison du Roi/Broodhuis), the square is a masterpiece of Gothic, Baroque, and Louis XIV architectural styles.

CASTLE OF THE COUNTS

The Castle of the Counts, known locally as Gravensteen, is a medieval fortress located in the heart of Ghent, Belgium. Built in 1180 by Count Philip of Alsace, it served as the residence of the Counts of Flanders and a symbol of their power.

BELFORT

The Belfort, or Belfry of Ghent, is one of the city’s most iconic medieval landmarks. Standing 91 meters tall, it has watched over the city since the 14th century and symbolizes Ghent’s independence and prosperity during the Middle Ages. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it’s part of a group of historic belfries found throughout Belgium and northern France.
